His journey reflects a deep engagement with the sport, marked by both on-field performance and strategic leadership roles. From being a late-round draft pick to managing a record-setting team, Kapler's trajectory offers insights into the multifaceted demands of professional baseball.

## What happened
Gabe Kapler was drafted by the Detroit Tigers in the 57th round of the 1995 MLB draft, an exceptionally late pick. Despite this, he quickly rose through the minor league ranks, earning All-Star recognition in 1996, 1997, and 1998, and being named Minor League Player of the Year in 1998 by national publications. His major league playing career spanned from 1998 to 2010, including stints with teams like the Boston Red Sox and Texas Rangers, and even a season with the Yomiuri Giants in Japan.

Following his playing days, Kapler transitioned into management and executive roles. He served as Director of Player Development for the Dodgers from 2014 to 2017, then managed the Philadelphia Phillies for two seasons. His most notable managerial success came with the San Francisco Giants in 2021, where he led the team to a franchise-record 107 wins and earned the National League Manager of the Year award. After being fired by the Giants in late 2023, he joined the Miami Marlins as an assistant general manager.
