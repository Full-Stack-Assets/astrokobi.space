Beloved British astronomer Guy Ottewell celebrated his 90th birthday on July 4, 2026, marking a significant milestone in his illustrious career. This year also brings a bittersweet announcement: 2026 will be the final edition of his iconic, hand-illustrated Astronomical Calendar. For over five decades, Ottewell's unique charts have served as an invaluable guide for millions of sky-lovers and astronomy educators worldwide, shaping how generations understood the cosmos.

## What happened
On July 4, 2026, British astronomer Guy Ottewell reached his 90th birthday, a momentous occasion for a figure whose work has profoundly influenced amateur and professional astronomy alike. This milestone coincides with the announcement that the 2026 edition will be the last of his renowned Astronomical Calendar, concluding a publication run that spanned over five decades.

First published in 1974, Ottewell's hand-illustrated calendar quickly became a staple, selling to thousands in over 100 countries until its initial hiatus in 2017. It saw a brief return in 2023 in both print and electronic formats, but the upcoming 2026 edition will definitively mark its conclusion. His distinctive charts, often featured in publications like EarthSky, have been a cornerstone for astronomy educators and a source of wonder for millions seeking to understand celestial mechanics and events.

Ottewell's path to creating the calendar was unique, stemming from a diverse background in languages, archaeology, and teaching, including at a Navajo school where he first used a telescope. Encouraged by a university professor, he developed the astronomical yearbook, teaching himself complex trigonometry and computer programming to bring his vision to life, eventually expanding his publishing imprint to include other interests.

## Why it matters
The cessation of Guy Ottewell's Astronomical Calendar signifies more than just the end of a publication; it marks the close of an era for a unique form of astronomy education. His calendar wasn't merely a collection of data; it was a beautifully rendered, personally crafted guide that demystified the cosmos for countless individuals. Educators will lose a trusted, visual aid that simplified complex celestial movements, while casual sky-watchers will miss a reliable companion for planning observations and deepening their understanding of the night sky.

Ottewell's work stood apart by blending scientific rigor with artistic elegance, a testament to his multidisciplinary background. Its discontinuation highlights the challenge of maintaining such specialized, passion-driven projects in a rapidly evolving digital landscape. His legacy will endure through the millions he inspired and the fundamental understanding of our universe he fostered, but the tangible, annual anticipation of his new calendar will be deeply missed by a dedicated global community.
